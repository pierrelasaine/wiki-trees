Japanese Magnolia

History

Japanese Magnolias (Magnolia x soulangeana) are a hybrid of two Asian Magnolia species, and were first cultivated in France in the 1820s. They were later introduced to the United States, where they quickly became a popular ornamental tree. Today, Japanese Magnolias are a common sight in parks, gardens, and other landscaped areas throughout the United States.

Biology

Japanese Magnolias are small to medium-sized trees that typically grow to a height of 20 to 30 feet. They have large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically pink or white, and can be up to 10 inches in diameter. Japanese Magnolias have smooth, gray bark and ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

Fun Facts

Japanese Magnolias are sometimes called Tulip Magnolias because of the shape and color of their flowers. They are also sometimes referred to as Saucer Magnolias. Japanese Magnolias are a popular subject of photography and artwork, and are often used as a symbol of spring and renewal.
