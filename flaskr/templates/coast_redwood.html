Coast Redwood

History

Coast Redwood (Sequoia sempervirens) is a species of coniferous tree that is native to the Pacific coast of the United States. It has been an important part of the region's history and culture for centuries, and has been used for everything from building materials to medicinal purposes. Today, Coast Redwood is a popular ornamental tree and can be found in parks, gardens, and other landscaped areas throughout the United States.

Biology

Coast Redwood is a large evergreen tree that can grow to a height of 300 feet or more. It has a tall, straight trunk and a broad, conical shape. The leaves are small and scale-like, and the bark of the tree is thick and reddish-brown. Coast Redwood produces small cones that are about an inch in length.

Coast Redwood is sometimes called the "Sequoia" after the giant sequoia, another species of tree in the same family. It is also sometimes referred to as the "California Redwood" because of its location on the California coast.
